Japan Migration

Migration

Over the past several hundred years, there has been much migration of people from countries such as China, the United States, Russia, and the United Kingdom.

Internal Migration

Recently, due to urbanization, people have been moving from the rural farming areas of Japan toward the larger city centers where business and commerce take place.

Push Factors

Push factors, which are negative conditions that make a migrant want to leave their home country in search of another, occur in Japan.

  • War
  • Financial problems for research and research grants
  • Natural Diasters

Pull Factors

Pull factors, which are positive conditions that make a migrant want to leave their home country in search of another, also occur in Japan.

  • High paying jobs
  • Financial problems for research and research grants
  • Stable economy
